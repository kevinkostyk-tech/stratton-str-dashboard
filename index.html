The STR Loophole in plain English: Because your average guest stay is ≤7 days, the IRS treats this like a business (not a passive rental). That means depreciation losses offset your W-2 income directly — no real estate professional status required. Year 1 is the big year: 100% bonus depreciation on the reclassified short-life assets creates a massive paper loss that reduces your taxable income immediately.
How the Tax Stack Works

📐 Step 1 — Cost Segregation

🏗️
Hire an engineer to reclassify building components into 5-yr, 15-yr, and 39-yr buckets
Flooring, fixtures, cabinetry, hot tub, landscaping, driveway, deck → shorter life
📊
Typical result for ski home: 25–30% of building value reclassified
We model 25% (conservative)
💵
Cost of study: ~$5,000–$8,000 (one-time)
Pays for itself in first-year tax savings many times over

⚡ Step 2 — 100% Bonus Depreciation

✅
All reclassified short-life assets deducted 100% in Year 1
Restored under 2025 legislation for property placed in service after Jan 19, 2025
📉
Creates large paper loss in Year 1
No cash outflow — pure accounting acceleration
📋
Remaining 75% of building still depreciates over 39 years (straight-line)
Generates annual deduction in Years 2–39

💼 Step 3 — Non-Passive Loss Offset

🔑
≤7-day avg guest stay + material participation = losses are non-passive
Offsets W-2 income dollar-for-dollar (no $25K cap, no REPS required)
📈
Additional operating deductions every year
Mortgage interest, property taxes, STR license, insurance, repairs, utilities (rental %)
🏖️
Personal use days require proration
4–6 weeks personal = ~85% rental allocation for deductions

Understanding the ARM Risk
7/1 ARM — What Actually Happens in Year 8?
The rate is fixed at 6.375% for 7 years (84 payments). Starting in month 85, it adjusts annually based on a benchmark index (typically SOFR) + a margin, subject to caps.

Typical ARM caps structure: 2/2/5 — meaning the first adjustment can't exceed +2%, subsequent annual adjustments can't exceed +2%, and the lifetime cap is +5% above the start rate.

Worst-case Year 8 rate: 11.375% (6.375% + 5% lifetime cap) → P&I would rise to ~$7,700/mo. Extreme scenario; unlikely absent a major rate shock.
