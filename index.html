LTR Close Strategy — Why & How

Closing as Long-Term Rental (LTR) is fully legal. Vermont imposes a higher transfer tax on STR-intent closings. By demonstrating genuine long-term rental use in Year 1, you qualify for the lower rate.

1
Close as LTR — declare intent as primary/LTR at closing. Pay 1.47% transfer tax rate.
2
Execute 2–3 genuine 30-day rentals — real tenants with real leases. These tenants double as caretakers during shoulder season.
3
Document everything — signed leases, rent receipts, correspondence. Vermont looks at actual use, not just intent.
4
Convert to STR in Year 2 — obtain STR license, list on Airbnb/VRBO, engage property manager.
5
Commission cost seg study — Year 2 trigger. 25% of $696K building value = $174K eligible → 100% bonus dep = $60,900 tax benefit. Remaining $522K straight-line over 39yr = $4,685/yr.
